Sir Isaac Newton

Learn about the life and works of Isaac Newton

Time Line
  • January 4, 1643

    Isaac Newton is born in Woolsthorpe, Lincolnshire, England.

  • 1653

    He is sent to grammar school in Grantham, England, to prepare for university.

  • 1661

    Newton begins his undergraduate studies at Trinity College, Cambridge.

  • 1664

    Newton writes a set of notes, which he entitles “Quaestiones Quaedam Philosophicae” (“Certain Philosophical Questions”).

  • 1665–67

    Newton receives his bachelor’s degree in 1665, but the mathematical and scientific studies he has undertaken during his undergraduate years are still unknown to the world.

  • 1668

    Newton invents the reflecting telescope.

  • 1669–71

    Newton becomes a professor of mathematics at Trinity College and writes the tract De Analysi per Aequationes Numero Terminorum Infinitas (“On Analysis by Equations with an Infinite Number of Terms”) in 1669.

  • 1672

    Newton sends a paper on light and colors to the Royal Society.

  • 1684–87

    Toward the end of 1684 Newton sends a short tract entitled De Motu (“On Motion”) to British astronomer Edmond Halley.

  • 1696

    Newton is appointed warden of the mint and moves to London (although he does not resign from Cambridge until 1701).

  • 1703

    Newton is elected president of the Royal Society.

  • 1704

    Newton publishes the first edition of Opticks, a work summarizing his research in light and colors.

  • 1705

    Queen Anne knights Newton, the first time a scientist receives the honor.

  • March 31, 1727

    Newton dies in London, England, and is buried at Westminster Abbey.
